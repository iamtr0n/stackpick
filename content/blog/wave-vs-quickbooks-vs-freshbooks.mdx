Invoicing and accounting software isn't the most exciting purchase you'll make, but it might be the most important. Late invoices kill cash flow. Messy books make tax season a nightmare. The right tool runs in the background and just works.

We compared Wave, QuickBooks, and FreshBooks — the three most popular options for small businesses — to help you pick the right one for your situation.

**The short version:**
- **Wave** — Best for freelancers and very small businesses (free, but limited)
- **QuickBooks** — Best for growing businesses with employees and complex finances
- **FreshBooks** — Best for service businesses and agencies billing hourly

Now let's dig into why.

---

## Wave — Free Accounting for Freelancers

Wave is a genuinely free accounting platform with no catch (for the core features). It's been offering free accounting and invoicing to small businesses since 2010, and it's legitimately capable.

### What Wave Does Well

**Free forever — for real.** Invoicing, accounting, and receipt scanning are completely free. Wave makes money on payroll (add-on) and payment processing (fee per transaction). The free accounting features have no artificial limits.

**Clean, approachable UI.** Wave is designed for non-accountants. The dashboard is clear, income and expense tracking is straightforward, and reports are easy to read.

**Solid invoicing.** Professional invoice templates, automatic payment reminders, and online payment acceptance (credit card via Wave Payments) make invoicing easy.

**Double-entry accounting.** Real bookkeeping under the hood — not just cash-flow tracking. This makes your P&L, balance sheet, and tax prep clean.

### Wave's Limitations

**No inventory management.** If you sell physical products, Wave isn't built for you.

**Limited integrations.** Wave doesn't connect with Shopify, Stripe, or most SaaS tools you might use. Zapier support is limited.

**No time tracking.** You'll need a separate tool for tracking billable hours.

**Payroll is an add-on.** $35/month base + $6/employee. Not bad, but it removes Wave from the "free" category if you have employees.

**Support is limited.** No phone support. Email and chat support quality has mixed reviews.

### Wave Pricing

| Feature | Price |
|---|---|
| Accounting & Invoicing | Free |
| Receipt Scanning | Free |
| Payroll (US/Canada) | $40/month + $6/employee |
| Payment Processing | 2.9% + $0.60 per transaction |

**Best for:** Freelancers, consultants, solo operators, and very small businesses with simple finances and no employees.

## QuickBooks Online — The Industry Standard

QuickBooks Online is the most widely used small business accounting software in the world, and there's a reason every accountant knows it. It's powerful, thorough, and deeply integrated with the financial ecosystem.

### What QuickBooks Does Well

**Complete accounting.** Invoicing, bill payment, expense tracking, payroll, inventory, job costing, sales tax — it's all there. QuickBooks is a genuine accounting platform, not just an invoice tool.

**Bank reconciliation is seamless.** Connect your bank and credit cards, and QuickBooks automatically categorizes transactions and flags discrepancies. Month-end reconciliation takes minutes.

**Your accountant will love you.** When tax season comes, QuickBooks exports exactly what your CPA needs. Almost every accounting firm knows QuickBooks inside and out.

**Payroll integration.** QuickBooks Payroll integrates natively — no data entry required to run payroll accurately.

**Inventory management.** The Plus plan includes inventory tracking, which Wave and FreshBooks lack.

**Reporting depth.** 80+ pre-built reports, plus customizable dashboards. P&L, balance sheet, cash flow, AR aging — all clean and exportable.

### QuickBooks Limitations

**Price creep.** QuickBooks starts at $35/month but you'll almost certainly need the Plus plan ($90/month). Add payroll ($75+ /month extra) and it gets expensive fast.

**UI is dated.** The desktop-era design shows. It works, but it's not a joy to use.

**Learning curve.** More powerful = more complex. Plan on a few hours of learning, or budget for a bookkeeper.

**Customer support is inconsistent.** Long hold times and varying rep quality have frustrated many users.

### QuickBooks Online Pricing

| Plan | Price | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| Simple Start | $35/month | 1 user, basic invoicing, bank sync |
| Essentials | $65/month | 3 users, bill management, time tracking |
| Plus | $90/month | 5 users, inventory, project tracking |
| Advanced | $200/month | 25 users, custom reporting, dedicated support |

*Note: QuickBooks frequently runs 50% off promotions for new subscribers — always check for current deals.*

**Best for:** Growing businesses with employees, physical inventory, complex tax situations, or anyone whose accountant already uses QuickBooks.

## FreshBooks — Best for Service Businesses

FreshBooks carved out its niche by making invoicing beautiful and building the best time-tracking integration of any accounting tool. It's designed for service businesses — consultants, agencies, designers, lawyers — that bill by the hour.

### What FreshBooks Does Well

**The best invoicing experience.** FreshBooks invoices are beautiful, customizable, and client-friendly. Online payment, automatic reminders, and client preview links make getting paid faster and easier.

**Time tracking is first-class.** Built-in timers, project time tracking, and automatic invoice generation from time logs. No more manually tallying up hours.

**Project profitability.** See exactly how profitable each project or client is — billable hours vs. time spent, expenses, and invoiced amounts all in one view.

**Client portal.** Clients can log in to view invoices, pay, and communicate through FreshBooks. Great for ongoing client relationships.

**Clean, modern UI.** One of the most intuitive interfaces in the accounting space.

### FreshBooks Limitations

**Not a full accounting platform.** FreshBooks is strong on invoicing and expense tracking, weaker on true accounting depth. Bank reconciliation and journal entries exist, but aren't as robust as QuickBooks.

**No inventory.** Like Wave, FreshBooks isn't built for product businesses.

**Client limits.** Each plan limits how many clients you can have active — annoying if you have lots of small clients.

**Pricier than Wave.** The free option doesn't exist.

### FreshBooks Pricing

| Plus | $33/month | 50 clients |
| Premium | $60/month | Unlimited clients |
| Select | Custom | Unlimited + dedicated support |

**Best for:** Freelancers, consultants, agencies, and service businesses that bill hourly and want beautiful invoices with strong time tracking.

## Which One Is Right for You?

**Choose Wave if:**
- You're a freelancer or solo operator with simple finances
- You're just starting out and want to keep costs at zero
- You don't have employees or inventory
- You're comfortable with limited integrations and support

**Choose QuickBooks if:**
- You have employees and run payroll
- You sell physical products and need inventory tracking
- You work with an accountant or bookkeeper (they'll likely prefer QuickBooks)
- You need robust reporting for business decisions or loan applications
- You're growing and expect financial complexity to increase

**Choose FreshBooks if:**
- You're a service business: consultant, designer, lawyer, agency, contractor
- You bill clients by the hour and need time tracking
- You want the cleanest, most modern invoicing experience
- Client communication and professionalism on invoices matters to you
- You have ongoing client relationships you want to manage in one place

---

## The Bottom Line

All three tools are solid. The choice really comes down to your business model:

**Freelancer with simple needs** → Wave (free is hard to beat)
**Growing product business** → QuickBooks (the accounting depth you'll need)
**Service business billing time** → FreshBooks (built specifically for you)

Don't overthink it. Any of these three will serve you better than a spreadsheet. Pick the one that fits your situation and start using it.
