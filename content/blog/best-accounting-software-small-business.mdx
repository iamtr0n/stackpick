Accounting software is one of the highest-stakes software decisions a small business makes. Get it wrong and you're either overpaying for features you don't need or spending extra hours on bookkeeping because your tool doesn't match how you work.

We've tested QuickBooks, FreshBooks, Wave, Xero, and Zoho Books with real small business scenarios. Here's the honest breakdown.

## 1. QuickBooks Online — The Industry Standard

<a href={QUICKBOOKS_AFFILIATE} target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Try QuickBooks Online →</a>

**Pricing:** Simple Start $35/month, Essentials $65/month, Plus $99/month, Advanced $235/month

QuickBooks is the 800-pound gorilla of small business accounting. It has ~80% market share among small businesses in the US for a reason: it's the most complete accounting tool available, your accountant almost certainly knows it, and it integrates with almost everything.

**What QuickBooks does better than anyone:**
- **Bank reconciliation** — connect your bank and credit cards, rules auto-categorize transactions
- **Tax preparation** — clean categorized data your accountant or tax software can actually use
- **Payroll integration** — QuickBooks Payroll (separate subscription) integrates seamlessly
- **Reporting** — profit & loss, balance sheet, cash flow, accounts receivable aging, and 50+ others
- **Inventory tracking** (Plus plan) — track inventory quantities and COGS automatically
- **Multi-user access** — Simple Start is 1 user, Essentials is 3 users, Plus is 5 users

**The honest downsides:**
- Price is the main complaint. $35/month for Simple Start is reasonable. But features you actually need for a real business often require Essentials ($65/month) or Plus ($99/month).
- The UI is functional but dated and can feel cluttered. Not the most intuitive tool.
- Customer support quality is inconsistent.

**Best for:** Any small business that pays taxes in the US, has inventory, or has an accountant. QuickBooks is the default choice if you don't have a specific reason to pick something else.

## 2. FreshBooks — Best for Service Businesses and Freelancers

<a href={FRESHBOOKS_AFFILIATE} target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Try FreshBooks →</a>

**Pricing:** Lite $19/month (5 clients), Plus $33/month (50 clients), Premium $60/month (unlimited), Select custom

FreshBooks was built for service businesses — consultants, freelancers, agencies, designers, contractors. It shows. Invoicing and time tracking are class-leading. The client experience is polished. And the interface is significantly easier to use than QuickBooks.

**Where FreshBooks wins:**
- **Invoicing** — Beautiful, customizable invoices. Automatic payment reminders. Late fees. One-click send. Clients can pay directly from the invoice.
- **Time tracking** — Built-in, with timers you run against specific projects and clients. Time converts directly to invoice line items.
- **Client portal** — Clients can see their invoices, project status, and communication history in a branded portal.
- **Proposal and contract tools** (Premium+) — Send proposals, get contracts signed, convert to invoice automatically.
- **Usability** — The cleanest UI in accounting software. Setup takes an hour. Learning curve is minimal.

**Where FreshBooks loses:**
- Double-entry accounting is less robust than QuickBooks. Not ideal if you have complex accounting needs.
- The client limit on lower plans is a real limitation. 5 clients on Lite is very restrictive.
- Inventory management is not a strength.
- Payroll requires a third-party integration.

**Best for:** Freelancers, consultants, agencies, and service-based businesses that invoice clients and track time. Anyone whose main accounting need is "I need to get paid and know where my money went."

## 3. Wave — Best Free Option

**Pricing:** Accounting, invoicing, and receipts: **Free forever.** Payroll: $40/month + $6/employee. Payments: 2.9% + 60¢ per card transaction.

Wave is genuinely free for accounting and invoicing — no trial, no credit card, no catch. The business model is payment processing fees and payroll subscriptions.

**What Wave includes free:**
- Double-entry accounting
- Unlimited invoicing (with online payment via credit card or ACH)
- Bank connections for transaction import
- Expense tracking and categorization
- Basic financial reports (P&L, balance sheet, cash flow)
- Unlimited users

**The honest limitations:**
- Customer support is paid (the "Wave Advisor" subscription for live chat/call support is $149/month — more than QuickBooks). Free tier is email-only.
- The feature set hasn't grown much in recent years. It feels like it's being maintained, not invested in.
- Inventory management doesn't exist.
- Time tracking doesn't exist.
- The mobile app is basic.

**Best for:** Freelancers, solopreneurs, and micro-businesses who need basic accounting without paying for software. Wave is completely legitimate for simple business finances.

## 4. Xero — Best for Growing Businesses with International Operations

**Pricing:** Early $20/month, Growing $47/month, Established $80/month

Xero is popular in the UK, Australia, and New Zealand — and gaining ground in the US. It's a serious QuickBooks competitor for businesses that need strong multi-currency support, international banking, or a more modern UI.

**Xero's strengths:**
- **Unlimited users on all plans** — Unlike QuickBooks, which charges per user, Xero includes unlimited users on every plan. Big deal for teams.
- **Multi-currency accounting** — Built-in and excellent (Established plan). QuickBooks charges more for this.
- **Bank reconciliation UX** — Many accountants prefer Xero's reconciliation experience to QuickBooks'.
- **Modern interface** — Cleaner and more intuitive than QuickBooks.
- **Xero App Marketplace** — 1,000+ integrations.

**Xero's limitations:**
- Less common in the US than QuickBooks. Your accountant may not know it.
- Payroll is handled via partner integration (Gusto, etc.) rather than native.
- Early plan limits you to 20 invoices and 5 bills per month — genuinely restrictive.
- Reporting is solid but not as deep as QuickBooks on lower plans.

**Best for:** Growing businesses with international customers/suppliers, businesses with large teams (Xero's unlimited users beats QuickBooks), and UK/Australia/NZ-based businesses.

---

## 5. Zoho Books — Best Budget Option

**Pricing:** Free (for businesses with <$50K/year revenue), Standard $20/month, Professional $50/month, Premium $70/month

Zoho Books is the accounting arm of the Zoho suite — the same company that makes Zoho CRM, Zoho Projects, and 40+ other business tools. If you're already in the Zoho ecosystem, Books integrates cleanly.

**Zoho Books strengths:**
- **Free plan is genuinely useful** for small revenue businesses (under $50K/year, 1 user, 1 accountant)
- Strong invoicing with client portal
- Good automation (recurring invoices, payment reminders)
- Project and time tracking on Professional+
- Integrates with Zoho CRM, Zoho Inventory, and the rest of the Zoho suite

**Zoho Books limitations:**
- Best value if you're already in Zoho. If you're not, the integrations are less compelling.
- US payroll support requires Zoho Payroll, which has limited geographic availability.
- Less accountant adoption than QuickBooks or Xero.

**Best for:** Businesses already using Zoho tools, and budget-conscious businesses who outgrow Wave but don't want to pay QuickBooks prices.

## Recommended By Business Type

**Freelancer or solo consultant:** Start with Wave (free) or FreshBooks ($19/month). QuickBooks is overkill.

**Service business (agency, firm, studio):** FreshBooks if invoicing and time tracking are central. QuickBooks if you have an accountant who prefers it or need payroll.

**Product-based business with inventory:** QuickBooks Plus. Inventory tracking is the reason.

**Growing business with a team:** Xero (unlimited users) or QuickBooks Essentials. Depends on your accountant's preference.

**Budget-constrained small business:** Wave to start, then Zoho Books or FreshBooks when you outgrow it.

---

## The Bottom Line

QuickBooks is the safe default — the most widely supported, most complete, and what your accountant knows. FreshBooks is the right choice if invoicing and client experience matter more than accounting depth. Wave is the legitimate free option for businesses that don't need payroll or time tracking.

Don't pick accounting software by what looks nicest. Pick by what your accountant knows and what your business actually needs.
