"Free email marketing" is a category full of misleading claims. Most tools either require a credit card (which means they can charge you when the trial ends), limit you to a laughably small number of emails, or bury the useful features behind a paywall.

We only included tools with genuinely free plans — no credit card required, no expiration date. Here's what each one actually gives you.

## 1. Mailchimp — Most Recognizable, Most Restricted Free Plan

**Free: 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month, 1 audience**

Mailchimp is the most recognized name in email marketing — but its free plan has been progressively restricted over the years. The 500-contact limit is low. 1,000 emails/month means you can email your list twice before you're out.

**What you get free:**
- Basic drag-and-drop email builder
- 1 audience (list)
- Single-step automations (welcome email only — not multi-step flows)
- Basic signup forms and landing pages
- Email and chat support (first 30 days only; email-only after)

**What's behind the paywall:**
- Multi-step automation sequences
- A/B testing
- Scheduled sends
- Additional audiences
- Remove Mailchimp branding (you'll see "Sent with Mailchimp" in the footer)

**The honest assessment:** Mailchimp's free plan was great 5 years ago. Today it's the most restricted free plan of the five tools here. The 500-contact limit is a real wall, and only single-step automation makes it hard to use for anything beyond basic newsletters.

**Best for:** Beginners just learning email marketing, or businesses with very small lists (under 300 contacts) who want a familiar brand.

---

## 2. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) — Best Free Plan for High Volume

**Free: Unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day (~9,000/month)**

Brevo's free plan is distinctive: unlimited contacts but capped daily sending (300 emails/day). This model is better for businesses with growing lists that don't send every day.

**What you get free:**
- Unlimited contact storage
- 300 emails/day (no monthly cap — just daily)
- Drag-and-drop email editor
- Basic automation workflows (up to 2,000 contacts per workflow)
- Transactional emails (useful for order confirmations, welcome emails via API)
- SMS marketing (pay per SMS)
- Basic landing pages
- Live chat widget for your website

**What's behind the paywall:**
- Unlimited daily sending (Starter plan removes the daily cap)
- Remove Brevo branding
- Phone support
- Advanced automation

**The honest assessment:** Brevo's daily limit is more business-friendly than contact limits if you have a large list but send infrequently. The unlimited contacts + automation + transactional email on the free plan is genuinely useful. The 300/day cap is the only real limitation — if you send a campaign to 1,000 people, it'll drip over 3+ days.

**Best for:** Businesses with a growing list that sends 1–2 times per week and can work within the daily sending limit.

## 3. MailerLite — Best Overall Free Plan

**Free: 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month, automation workflows**

MailerLite's free plan is the most generous of any tool on this list on a features-per-dollar (free) basis. You get real automation, a good email builder, and 12,000 emails/month — enough for 12 campaigns to your full list.

**What you get free:**
- 1,000 subscribers
- 12,000 emails/month
- Full automation workflows (multi-step, branching logic)
- Drag-and-drop email builder
- Landing pages (10 landing pages)
- Signup forms and pop-ups
- Basic analytics (opens, clicks, unsubscribes)
- 24/7 email support

**What's behind the paywall ($10/month Growing Business):**
- Remove MailerLite branding
- Custom HTML editor
- Newsletter templates (free plan has limited templates)
- Auto-resend to unopens
- More advanced reporting
- Unlimited landing pages

**The honest assessment:** MailerLite's free plan stands out because you get *real* automation — multi-step welcome sequences, subscriber tagging, conditional logic — not just a single welcome email like Mailchimp. For a business starting email marketing, 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month is enough to get real results before paying.

The branding in the email footer is the main downside. If your audience is sophisticated (B2B, professional services), it looks slightly unprofessional to have "Powered by MailerLite" in the footer.

**Best for:** Businesses just starting email marketing who want a real free plan with automation. This is our top pick for free email marketing.

## 4. HubSpot Email Marketing — Best Free Plan for CRM Users

**Free: Unlimited contacts, 2,000 emails/month, basic automation**

HubSpot includes email marketing in its free CRM — and the free email feature is better than most people realize.

**What you get free:**
- 2,000 marketing emails/month
- Unlimited contacts (HubSpot CRM contacts)
- Drag-and-drop email builder
- Personalization tokens (use contact properties in email content)
- Basic email automation (triggered from contact properties or form submissions)
- Email analytics (opens, clicks, bounces, unsubscribes)
- List segmentation based on contact properties

**What's behind the paywall:**
- Removing HubSpot branding
- A/B testing
- Advanced automation sequences (Smart Rules, workflows)
- Email scheduling
- Higher sending limits

**The honest assessment:** If you're already using HubSpot CRM (which is free with unlimited users), the email marketing integration is powerful. Your email open and click data connects directly to contact records. Segments are pulled from your CRM data. You can personalize emails with contact properties easily.

The 2,000/month limit is real — you can only email your full list a couple of times per month if it's 1,000+ contacts. But for CRM-focused businesses that want email tied to their pipeline, HubSpot free is hard to beat.

**Best for:** Businesses already using HubSpot CRM who want email marketing that connects to their sales pipeline.

## 5. Zoho Campaigns — Best Free Option in the Zoho Ecosystem

**Free: 2,000 contacts, 6,000 emails/month, basic automation**

Zoho Campaigns is the email marketing tool in the Zoho suite. Like HubSpot, it's designed to work alongside a CRM (Zoho CRM) and other business tools.

**What you get free:**
- 2,000 contacts
- 6,000 emails/month
- Email templates
- Signup forms
- Basic automation workflows
- Basic reports
- Integration with Zoho CRM

**What's behind the paywall:**
- Advanced segmentation
- A/B testing
- Remove Zoho branding
- Advanced automation
- Phone support

**The honest assessment:** Zoho Campaigns' free plan is solid — 2,000 contacts and 6,000 emails/month is workable for small businesses. The tool shines most if you're in the Zoho ecosystem, where it integrates natively with Zoho CRM, Zoho Books, and other Zoho tools. Standalone, it's competitive but not the top pick.

**Best for:** Businesses already using Zoho CRM or other Zoho tools.

## The Winner: MailerLite

MailerLite wins the free email marketing category because it gives you:
- Enough contacts (1,000) to get started and get results
- Enough emails (12,000/month) for multiple campaigns
- **Real multi-step automation** — something Mailchimp's free plan doesn't offer
- Consistently good deliverability
- 24/7 email support

The Mailchimp name recognition fades when you see the plan limitations. For a free tool that actually helps you build an email marketing program — not just collect addresses — MailerLite is the pick.

**Runner-up: Brevo** for businesses with a large list that grows faster than they send (unlimited contacts).

**Runner-up: HubSpot** if you're already in the HubSpot ecosystem and want email tied to your CRM.

## When to Stop Using a Free Plan

The moment you should upgrade:

1. **Your list grows past the contact limit** — upgrading is usually $10–20/month, well worth it
2. **You want to remove branding** — for professional or B2B businesses, "Powered by MailerLite" matters
3. **You need automation sequences** — free Mailchimp doesn't have them; paid MailerLite ($10/month) unlocks everything
4. **Deliverability is a concern** — free plans often share IP addresses; paid plans get dedicated IPs

Paid email marketing starts at $10/month for most tools. It's one of the best ROI investments in marketing if you use it. Start free, prove the channel works, then upgrade.
