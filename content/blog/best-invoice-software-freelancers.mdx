Invoicing is one of those things that sounds trivial until you're chasing down a late payment, trying to remember which client owes you what, and manually calculating tax on a Sunday night. The right invoice software eliminates all of that.

We tested the five most popular invoicing tools for freelancers in 2025. Here's what actually works — and who should use each one.

## What Freelancers Actually Need in Invoice Software

Forget enterprise feature lists. As a freelancer, you need:

- **Professional-looking invoices** that don't embarrass you in front of clients
- **Online payment acceptance** — Stripe, PayPal, or ACH so clients pay faster
- **Expense tracking** (if you bill for expenses or need tax deductions)
- **Recurring invoices** for retainer clients
- **Late payment reminders** that send automatically
- **A price you can stomach** when you're just getting started

## The Five Best Invoice Tools for Freelancers

### 1. FreshBooks — Best Overall for Freelancers

FreshBooks was built with freelancers and small service businesses in mind, and it shows. The invoicing experience is polished, the automation is genuinely useful, and the time tracking is tight enough that you can run your entire billing workflow in one place.

**What makes it exceptional:**
- Clean, professional invoice templates that clients actually compliment
- Built-in time tracking that auto-populates invoices — no manual entry
- Automatic payment reminders that run on a schedule you set
- Accept credit cards, ACH, and PayPal directly on invoices
- Real double-entry accounting under the hood — not just a pretty ledger
- Solid iOS and Android apps for invoicing on the go
- Proposals and contracts on higher tiers

**Pricing:**
- Lite: $19/month (5 active clients)
- Plus: $33/month (50 active clients)
- Premium: $60/month (unlimited clients)
- 30-day free trial, no credit card required

**Best for:** Freelancers billing 5+ clients regularly who want professional invoicing with real accounting built in.

**Watch out:** The Lite plan's 5-client cap sounds limiting but covers most solo freelancers just fine. You only count clients with active invoices, not all-time clients.

---

### 2. Wave — Best Free Option

Wave is completely free for invoicing and accounting. Not a free trial. Not a free tier with crippled features. Fully free, forever, for invoicing and bookkeeping.

**What makes it exceptional:**
- $0 for unlimited invoices, unlimited clients, unlimited expenses
- Double-entry accounting included at no cost
- Connect your bank accounts for automatic transaction import
- Recurring invoices and automatic payment reminders
- Professional templates that don't look cheap
- Wave Payments lets clients pay by card (2.9% + $0.60 per transaction — you only pay when paid)

**Pricing:**
- Invoicing + Accounting: Free
- Wave Payments: 2.9% + $0.60 (card), 1% ACH (min $1)
- Wave Payroll: $20/month base + $6/employee

**Best for:** New freelancers, part-time freelancers, and anyone whose billing volume doesn't justify a monthly SaaS fee.

**Watch out:** Wave's customer support is limited on the free plan. If something breaks, you're mostly self-solving. Also, Wave lacks time tracking and project management — you'll need a separate tool if those matter.

---

### 3. HoneyBook — Best for Client-Facing Experience

HoneyBook isn't just invoicing — it's a full client management platform. If you have discovery calls, proposals, contracts, and invoices all tied to a single client journey, HoneyBook turns that mess into a streamlined workflow.

**What makes it exceptional:**
- End-to-end client workflow: inquiry → proposal → contract → invoice → payment
- Clients get a branded portal to review documents, sign contracts, and pay
- Smart files let you bundle proposals, contracts, and invoices into one document
- Automation can trigger follow-ups, invoice reminders, and thank-you emails
- Built-in scheduler for booking discovery calls
- Excellent mobile app

**Pricing:**
- Starter: $19/month (first 6 months, then $39)
- Essentials: $39/month
- Premium: $79/month

**Best for:** Freelancers in service industries — photographers, designers, consultants, coaches, event planners — where client experience and contracts matter as much as the invoice itself.

**Watch out:** HoneyBook is overkill if you just need to send invoices. It's a client management platform that includes invoicing, not primarily an invoicing tool. The learning curve reflects that.

---

### 4. Bonsai — Best for Freelancers Who Need Everything

Bonsai is the closest thing to a one-stop shop specifically designed for freelancers. Contracts, proposals, invoices, time tracking, project management, CRM, and taxes — all in one product.

**What makes it exceptional:**
- Pre-built freelance contract templates written by lawyers
- E-signatures built in for proposals and contracts
- Time tracking tied to projects and clients for accurate billing
- Invoice from tracked time in two clicks
- Tax estimation that tracks quarterly estimated taxes automatically
- Client CRM so you never lose track of who you've worked with

**Pricing:**
- Starter: $21/month (3 clients)
- Professional: $32/month (unlimited clients)
- Business: $52/month (unlimited + subcontractors)

**Best for:** Full-time freelancers who want a single platform instead of stitching together four separate tools.

**Watch out:** If you're already happy with your stack (separate contract tool, project management, etc.), Bonsai might be paying for features you don't need.

---

### 5. PayPal Invoicing — Best for Occasional Use

PayPal's free invoicing is often overlooked because it comes bundled with an already-ubiquitous payment platform. For freelancers who invoice rarely and have clients who already pay via PayPal, it's the path of least resistance.

**What makes it exceptional:**
- Zero monthly fee — completely free
- Clients already know and trust PayPal
- Partial payments and tipping supported
- Invoice tracking and status in the PayPal dashboard
- Multi-currency support for international clients

**Pricing:**
- Invoicing: Free
- Payment processing: 3.49% + $0.49 per transaction (PayPal balance), 3.49% + $0.49 (card)

**Best for:** Freelancers with occasional clients who want no-fuss invoicing without a monthly commitment. Especially good if your clients are already in the PayPal ecosystem.

**Watch out:** PayPal's transaction fees are higher than Wave Payments or Stripe. For high-volume billing, this adds up fast. Also, there's no accounting, time tracking, or expense management — PayPal is invoicing only.

## Our Pick: FreshBooks

For most freelancers, **FreshBooks is the best invoicing software in 2025**. The $19/month Lite plan covers everything a solo freelancer needs: professional invoices, time tracking, expense tracking, payment acceptance, and automatic reminders.

Here's the one-line decision tree:

- **Zero budget** → **Wave**. It's free and legitimately powerful. The lack of time tracking and project integration is a real gap, but if you don't need those, you'll never pay a cent.
- **Clients need contracts + a client portal** → **HoneyBook**. The workflow from inquiry to paid invoice is the best in class for service-based freelancers.
- **Want everything in one place** → **Bonsai**. The all-in-one approach saves tool-switching overhead for full-time freelancers.
- **Invoice once in a while** → **PayPal**. Free, familiar, and zero learning curve.
- **Everyone else** → **FreshBooks**. Best balance of professional invoicing, real accounting, and sensible pricing.
