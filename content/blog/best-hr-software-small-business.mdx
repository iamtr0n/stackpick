Running HR for a small business is genuinely hard. You're dealing with payroll taxes, new hire paperwork, benefits administration, time tracking, and compliance — all while trying to actually run your business. The right HR software handles the administrative burden so you can focus on people instead of paperwork.

The challenge: most "HR software" is built for enterprise companies with HR departments. Small businesses need something that's powerful enough to handle compliance but simple enough that a non-HR person can run it. Here's what actually works.

---

## What Small Businesses Actually Need from HR Software

Before the list, let's be clear about what matters for a sub-50-person business:

1. **Payroll** — accurate, tax-compliant, on-time. This is table stakes.
2. **New hire onboarding** — I-9, W-4, direct deposit setup, employee handbook acknowledgment
3. **Benefits administration** — health insurance, 401(k) enrollment and management
4. **Time tracking** — especially for hourly employees
5. **Compliance** — state and federal labor law changes, ACA reporting, etc.
6. **PTO tracking** — who's off, how much balance people have

Nice to have: performance reviews, HR document storage, employee self-service, org chart.

## 2. Rippling — Best for Tech-Forward Teams

**Pricing:** Starts ~$8/person/month (varies by modules — payroll, HR, IT management priced separately)

Rippling is the most powerful HR platform on this list — but "most powerful" comes with "most complex" and "most expensive." Rippling is an HR + IT management platform: it handles employee onboarding and simultaneously provisions their laptop, sets up their email, and adds them to the right Slack channels. When someone leaves, one offboarding click revokes all access.

For a 30-person tech startup where IT and HR blur together, Rippling is exceptional. For a 10-person retail shop, it's overkill and expensive.

**What makes Rippling different:**
- Unified HR + IT: device management, app provisioning, HRIS all in one
- Time tracking, recruiting, PTO in one platform
- Very configurable workflows and permissions
- Strong reporting and compliance

**Limitation:** Rippling's modular pricing can add up quickly. And the setup complexity is real — budget time for implementation.

**Best for:** Tech companies, SaaS businesses, and teams where IT administration and HR are closely related.

## 3. BambooHR — Best for HR-Focused Teams

**Pricing:** Essentials ~$6/employee/month, Advantage ~$9/employee/month (exact pricing requires a quote)

BambooHR is the gold standard for pure HR management — everything from hiring to performance to employee data. What it doesn't include natively: payroll (it integrates with Gusto, ADP, or Paychex) and benefits administration.

**BambooHR strengths:**
- **Hiring and ATS** — job posting, applicant tracking, interview scheduling, offer letters
- **Onboarding checklists** — assign tasks to new hires, managers, and IT
- **Performance management** — 360 reviews, goal tracking, self-assessments
- **Employee records** — the single source of truth for all HR data
- **Time tracking and PTO** — clean and easy to use
- **Employee self-service** — employees update their own info, request PTO, view documents

**The catch:** You need to bring your own payroll. BambooHR integrates with Gusto, but you're paying for both. For small businesses that just need payroll + basic HR, Gusto alone is simpler and cheaper. BambooHR makes more sense when you're doing active hiring, performance management, and need a serious HR information system.

**Best for:** Growing businesses (20–100 people) that are doing real hiring and performance management, where HR is a defined function.

## 4. Paychex — Best for Businesses That Want a Human in the Loop

**Pricing:** Flex Essential starts ~$39/month + $5/employee/month (pricing requires quote for full features)

Paychex is one of the oldest payroll companies in the US and handles everything from 1-person businesses to large enterprises. The main differentiator from Gusto: **dedicated human support**. Paychex assigns you a dedicated payroll specialist who knows your account.

For business owners who aren't comfortable with DIY payroll and want a person to call, Paychex is worth the higher price.

**Paychex strengths:**
- Dedicated payroll specialist (not just a support ticket)
- Very strong compliance expertise
- HR advisory services available
- Handles multi-state payroll compliantly
- Good benefits administration

**Limitations:** Interface is dated. Pricing is less transparent (you need a quote). Overkill for businesses under 10 employees who are comfortable with Gusto.

**Best for:** Business owners who want human payroll support, businesses in complex multi-state situations, or companies that have had payroll problems and want a safety net.

## 5. Homebase — Best Free Option for Hourly Workers

**Pricing:** Free (basic), Essentials $24.95/month/location, Plus $59.95/month/location, All-in-One $99.95/month/location

Homebase is purpose-built for businesses with hourly employees: restaurants, retail, salons, gyms, and similar. Its core features are scheduling and time tracking — and the free plan is legitimately useful.

**Free plan includes:**
- Employee scheduling with shift assignments
- Time tracking (clock in/out via app or POS)
- Team messaging
- Basic HR documents

**Paid plans add:**
- Payroll (Basic requires Plus plan)
- Hiring tools
- PTO tracking
- Labor cost reporting
- Onboarding

**Limitation:** Homebase is not a complete HR platform. The strength is scheduling and time tracking for hourly workers. For payroll and benefits, you'll integrate with Gusto or ADP.

**Best for:** Restaurants, retail, salons, and other businesses with hourly employees who need scheduling + time tracking and may be happy with a free tool.
