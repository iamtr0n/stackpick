FreshBooks is a frequent recommendation for small service businesses, and for good reason. It does invoicing, time tracking, and client communication better than any other tool in its price range. ## What FreshBooks Is (and Isn't)

FreshBooks is **invoicing-first accounting software**. It's built for service businesses — consultants, agencies, designers, coaches, contractors — that need to bill clients, track time, and understand their income and expenses. The accounting features exist and are real, but the center of gravity is the client and the invoice.

It is **not** the right tool if you:
- Sell physical products with inventory
- Have complex accounting needs (multiple entities, advanced COGS tracking)
- Need an accountant who insists on QuickBooks

It **is** the right tool if you:
- Invoice clients for time or fixed-price projects
- Want to look professional when billing
- Run a one-person or small-team service business
- Value ease of use over accounting depth

## Invoicing: Where FreshBooks Genuinely Wins

FreshBooks built its reputation on invoicing quality, and it holds up.

**Invoice creation:** Fast. You can create and send a professional invoice in under 2 minutes. Templates are clean, and the customization options (logo, brand color, font, payment terms) are accessible without being overwhelming.

**Online payments:** Clients can pay directly from the invoice — credit card or ACH. The payment link is embedded in the invoice PDF and email. Payment confirmation is automatic, the invoice is marked paid, and your accountant can see it.

**Automatic reminders:** Set up payment reminders that go out automatically — before due, on due date, and after due date. Customize the message tone. This alone reduces late payments meaningfully.

**Late fees:** Add automatic late fees as a flat amount or percentage. The fee is calculated and added to the invoice automatically when the due date passes.

**Recurring invoices:** Set up an invoice to auto-send weekly, monthly, or on any custom schedule. Essential for retainer clients. The recurring invoice can auto-charge the client's card on file without them re-entering payment info.

**Invoice history:** Every client has a complete invoicing history with open, paid, and overdue invoices visible at a glance.

## Time Tracking

FreshBooks has one of the better built-in time tracking systems for a small business tool.

**How it works:**
- Start a timer from the app (desktop or mobile) or log time manually
- Tag time to a project and client
- Add a note describing what you worked on
- At billing time, pull unbilled hours directly into an invoice with one click

**The project context:** FreshBooks projects let you set a project budget, track expenses against it, and see profitability per project. Not as deep as a dedicated project management tool, but genuinely useful for understanding which clients and projects are worth your time.

**Team time tracking:** On Plus and Premium, team members can track their own time, you can approve timesheets, and the system tracks payroll expenses by project.

**What's missing:** No integration with timer apps like Toggl or Harvest on lower plans (though Zapier can connect them). If you already use a dedicated time tracking tool, you might have duplicate data.

## Client Portal

FreshBooks gives each client a branded portal where they can:
- View all their invoices (open, paid, and overdue)
- Make payments
- See project status and hours logged (if you enable it)
- Send messages to you

The portal URL is your business branded (something like yourbiz.freshbooks.com). Clients find it useful, and it reduces the "what's my invoice status?" support questions.

For service businesses with ongoing client relationships, the portal is a meaningful feature. It makes you look professional and reduces back-and-forth.

## Accounting Features

FreshBooks has the accounting features a service business actually needs:

**Expense tracking:** Connect bank accounts and credit cards. Import transactions, categorize them, and attach receipts (photo from mobile). Expenses flow into your profit & loss report.

**Financial reports:** P&L statement, tax summary, expense reports, accounts receivable aging, revenue by client. Not as deep as QuickBooks, but sufficient for a service business.

**Double-entry accounting:** FreshBooks added true double-entry accounting in 2019. Your accountant can get a complete picture. The journal entries are there, but FreshBooks keeps them mostly hidden from you (which is a feature, not a bug — you don't need to see them unless you're an accountant).

**Tax preparation:** The reports are clean enough for a CPA or tax software. Most service business accountants are comfortable working with FreshBooks exports.

**What's missing:** No inventory management, no class tracking, no bank reconciliation as smooth as QuickBooks. Multi-entity accounting is not supported.

The app is responsive and doesn't feel like a stripped-down version of the desktop product. Time tracking specifically works well on mobile — easy to start/stop timers when you're on client calls or in the field.

## Integrations

FreshBooks integrates with 100+ apps. The most relevant for small businesses:

Native integrations are limited — most are Zapier-based, which requires a Zapier subscription for complex workflows.
