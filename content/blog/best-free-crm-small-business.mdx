Free CRM software sounds great until you realize most of it isn't actually free. It's either a 14-day trial, a "freemium" tool with so many limits it's useless, or bait to get you on a sales call. This guide cuts through the noise.

We tested five CRMs with genuine free tiers — tools you can run a real business on without pulling out a credit card. Here's the honest breakdown.

## The Short Answer

**HubSpot CRM** is the best free CRM for most small businesses. It's legitimately free, not capped at 2 users, and the feature set beats what most tools charge $30/month for. The catch: HubSpot wants you to grow into their paid marketing and sales hubs, so the free tier is a long funnel. Still worth it.

---

## What Makes a CRM "Actually Free"?

Before the list, here's what we required:

- No credit card required to start
- No arbitrary 14-day expiration
- Usable by at least 2–3 people without paying
- Core features: contact management, deal tracking, activity logging

Tools that failed this test: Salesforce Essentials (trial only), Zoho CRM Plus (trial), Monday CRM (trial), Streak (too limited).

---

## The 5 Best Free CRMs for Small Business

### 1. HubSpot CRM — Best Overall Free CRM

**Free tier:** Unlimited users, unlimited contacts, 1 million contact storage limit (in practice, you'll never hit this)

HubSpot's free CRM is the most generous of any tool on this list — and it's not close. You get:

- **Contact & company records** with full activity timeline
- **Deal pipeline** (one pipeline, which is fine for most small businesses)
- **Email integration** with Gmail and Outlook — track opens, clicks, reply rates
- **Meeting scheduler** — share a booking link so prospects can book time directly
- **Live chat widget** for your website
- **Forms** to capture leads from your site
- **Basic reporting** — deal forecast, activity summary, funnel conversion

The catch is real though. HubSpot's free CRM is designed to upsell you into their Marketing Hub, Sales Hub, and Service Hub — all paid. You'll hit limits on email sequences (only 5 email sequences on free, up from nothing), automation, and reporting dashboards. If you want to send bulk emails or build multi-step nurture flows, you'll need to upgrade.

But for contact management, deal tracking, and basic pipeline visibility? The free tier is excellent and completely usable long-term.

**Best for:** Any small business that needs a proper CRM without paying. Especially good if you already use Gmail or Outlook.

**Not great for:** Businesses that need email automation from day one, or teams that hate being upsold.

---

### 2. Zoho CRM — Best Free Tier for Sales Teams Up to 3 Users

**Free tier:** 3 users, core CRM features, basic automation

Zoho CRM's free plan is legitimately useful — but only for teams of three or fewer. Once you hit user four, you're paying.

What you get free:
- Leads, contacts, accounts, deals modules
- Workflow automation (limited — 5 workflows max)
- Email integration
- Mobile app

Zoho's interface is more complex than HubSpot's, which cuts both ways. You get more configurability out of the box, but there's a steeper learning curve. If you have someone who likes setting up systems, Zoho rewards that effort. If you want plug-and-play, HubSpot is easier.

**Best for:** Solo founders or 2–3 person sales teams who want more customization than HubSpot offers.

**Not great for:** Teams of 4+, anyone who wants simplicity.

---

### 3. Freshsales (Freshworks CRM) — Best Free Tier for Contact Management

**Free tier:** Unlimited users, contacts, deals — but with significant limits

Freshsales' free plan (called "Free" on their pricing page) includes unlimited users and contacts, which is impressive. But the limits are real:

- No workflow automation
- No email sequences
- No AI-powered insights (Freddy AI is paid)
- Limited reporting — you get basic reports, nothing custom
- Phone/dialer is paid-only

The contact timeline is clean and easy to read. The deal pipeline works well. If you're a team that primarily needs a glorified address book with deal tracking and doesn't need automation, Freshsales free is solid.

**Best for:** Teams that want unlimited users at zero cost and are okay without automation.

**Not great for:** Anyone who needs workflow automation or email campaigns.

---

### 4. Bitrix24 — Most Features Free, Most Complex Free

**Free tier:** Unlimited users, contacts, deals — includes project management, HR tools, and more

Bitrix24 is the Swiss Army knife of free CRMs. It's aggressively feature-rich — almost overwhelmingly so. You get:

- Unlimited users
- CRM with full pipeline
- Project management (tasks, Kanban, Gantt)
- Built-in telephony
- HR tools and employee directory
- Website builder
- Live chat

The problem: Bitrix24 tries to be everything and succeeds at nothing exceptionally. The interface is dated, navigation is confusing, and feature bloat is real. Teams that need a simple CRM will find it overwhelming. Teams that want an all-in-one workspace might find it valuable.

The free cloud plan is hosted on Bitrix24's servers with a 5GB storage limit. Self-hosted is available but requires technical setup.

**Best for:** Teams that want a free all-in-one workspace (not just a CRM) and have the patience to learn it.

**Not great for:** Small teams that just want a clean, simple CRM.

---

### 5. Really Simple Systems (RSS) — Best Free CRM for B2B

**Free tier:** 2 users, 100 accounts, unlimited contacts per account

Really Simple Systems lives up to its name. It's one of the cleanest, most intuitive CRM interfaces available — and it's built specifically for B2B companies that sell to other businesses (accounts) rather than individual consumers.

The 100-account limit on the free plan is the main constraint. If you're selling to fewer than 100 companies, this is genuinely usable. Features include:

- Account and contact management
- Opportunity tracking
- Activity logging
- Email integration
- Custom fields
- Mobile app

If you outgrow 100 accounts, the Starter plan is $14/user/month — reasonable. But the free tier is a real product, not a trap.

**Best for:** B2B service businesses with a small number of key accounts (agencies, consultants, B2B SaaS).

**Not great for:** B2C businesses, e-commerce, or teams needing more than 2 users free.

## Which Free CRM Should You Pick?

**Pick HubSpot if:** You want the best overall free CRM with room to grow. It's the default recommendation for most small businesses.

**Pick Zoho if:** You need more customization than HubSpot, you're a team of 3 or fewer, and you don't mind a learning curve.

**Pick Freshsales if:** You have a large team (4+ people) and can't afford to pay, and you don't need automation yet.

**Pick Bitrix24 if:** You want project management and HR tools bundled with your CRM, and you have patience to learn a complex tool.

**Pick Really Simple Systems if:** You're a B2B business with fewer than 100 accounts and want the cleanest interface of any free CRM.

---

## The Honest Truth About Free CRMs

No free CRM is truly free forever — they're all built to convert you to paid. That's fine. The question is whether the free tier is genuinely useful or just a trial.

HubSpot, Freshsales, and Bitrix24 all have free tiers you can use indefinitely at no cost. Zoho and RSS have free tiers with real limits that will push you to paid eventually — but those paid plans are reasonably priced.

Start free, validate whether the tool works for your team, and upgrade when the limits actually become a problem. Don't over-engineer your CRM before you need to.
