Mailchimp was the default email marketing tool for a generation of small businesses. Then Intuit acquired it, raised prices several times, and quietly made the free plan less useful with each iteration. If you're on Mailchimp and your bill has crept up while the features haven't, you're not imagining it.

Here's who's winning the Mailchimp alternatives race in 2025 — and a straight answer on who should switch to what.

## Why People Are Leaving Mailchimp

The frustrations are specific and consistent:

**1. Pricing structure punishes growth.** Mailchimp charges by contact count, and it jumps in lumpy tiers. Going from 4,999 to 5,001 contacts can nearly double your monthly bill.

**2. Automation is limited on lower tiers.** Anything beyond basic autoresponders requires the Standard plan ($20/month for 500 contacts) or higher. Competitors give you full automation at lower price points.

**3. The free plan got worse.** In 2019, the Mailchimp free plan supported 2,000 subscribers and included marketing automation. Today, it's 500 contacts and no automation. That's not improvement.

**4. Customer support on lower tiers is minimal.** Email-only support with slow response times is a real pain when something breaks before a campaign launch.

**5. The interface feels dated.** Competitors have leapfrogged Mailchimp on UI quality, especially ConvertKit and ActiveCampaign.

## The Best Mailchimp Alternatives

### 1. ConvertKit (Kit) — Best for Creators and Solopreneurs

ConvertKit rebranded to Kit but the product is the same: the cleanest, most creator-focused email marketing platform available. If you're a blogger, course creator, coach, or anyone building an audience, ConvertKit is built for you.

**What makes it exceptional:**
- Subscriber-first model — tags and segments instead of lists (so no duplicate billing)
- Visual automation builder that's genuinely easy to understand
- Landing pages and forms included at all tiers
- Commerce features let you sell digital products directly through your list
- Plain-text email defaults that perform exceptionally well in deliverability
- Excellent integration with creator tools (Teachable, Gumroad, Kajabi, etc.)
- Free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers with forms and landing pages

**Pricing:**
- Free: Up to 1,000 subscribers (limited automations)
- Creator: $25/month for 1,000 subscribers ($50/month for 3K, $100/month for 10K)
- Creator Pro: $50/month for 1,000 subscribers

**Best for:** Bloggers, course creators, coaches, podcasters, and any solopreneur building an audience they want to monetize.

**Watch out:** ConvertKit's email template editor is intentionally minimal. It's good for text-heavy emails but limiting if your brand requires heavily designed newsletter formats.

---

### 2. ActiveCampaign — Best for Automation Depth

ActiveCampaign is the serious automation tool. If Mailchimp is a sprinkler, ActiveCampaign is a drip irrigation system — sophisticated, configurable, and capable of building email journeys of genuinely impressive complexity.

**What makes it exceptional:**
- The automation builder is the best in its class — conditional logic, branching paths, wait states, goals
- CRM built in at no extra cost — contacts have full deal pipeline functionality
- Lead scoring based on email behavior, site visits, purchase history
- Dynamic content blocks that change based on subscriber data
- SMS marketing built in
- Predictive sending determines optimal send times per subscriber
- Site tracking shows you what pages contacts visit

**Pricing:**
- Starter: $15/month for 1,000 contacts
- Plus: $49/month for 1,000 contacts
- Professional: $79/month for 1,000 contacts

**Best for:** E-commerce businesses, SaaS companies, and B2B marketers who need sophisticated behavioral automation. Also excellent for teams that want their CRM and email marketing in one tool.

**Watch out:** ActiveCampaign has a real learning curve. The power is undeniable but the onboarding demands investment. It's overkill for anyone who just wants to send a weekly newsletter.

---

### 3. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) — Best for Budget-First

Brevo's pricing model is fundamentally different from every other email platform: you pay by emails sent per day, not by contact count. This is a huge advantage if you have a large list but don't email them all constantly.

**What makes it exceptional:**
- Free plan includes unlimited contacts and up to 300 emails/day
- Paid plans are dramatically cheaper than Mailchimp for equivalent volume
- SMS marketing, WhatsApp campaigns, and transactional email all included
- Solid automation builder with multi-channel workflows
- Landing pages, signup forms, and a CRM included at all tiers
- GDPR-compliant data centers in the EU — relevant for European audiences

**Pricing:**
- Free: 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts
- Starter: $25/month (20K emails/month, no daily limit)
- Business: $65/month (20K emails + advanced automation)

**Best for:** Businesses with large contact lists who email infrequently, European businesses needing EU data residency, or anyone who wants serious functionality without serious price.

**Watch out:** Deliverability has historically been a weak point for Brevo compared to Mailchimp and ActiveCampaign. This has improved, but if deliverability is critical (e.g., transactional emails), run your own tests.

---

### 4. MailerLite — Best Mailchimp Replacement on a Budget

MailerLite is the closest "just like Mailchimp but cheaper and better" option. If you're on Mailchimp and want to leave without rethinking your entire email strategy, MailerLite is the path of least resistance.

**What makes it exceptional:**
- Clean, modern UI that's easier to use than Mailchimp
- Full automation included on the Growing Business plan
- Website and landing page builder included
- A/B testing on subject lines, send times, and content
- Free plan supports 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month
- Migration assistance and Mailchimp import built in

**Pricing:**
- Free: 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month (automation included)
- Growing Business: $13/month for 1,000 subscribers
- Advanced: $36/month for 1,000 subscribers

**Best for:** Mailchimp users who want to downsize their bill without a steep learning curve. Great for bloggers, small retailers, and nonprofits.

**Watch out:** MailerLite's automation is solid but not at ActiveCampaign's level. For complex behavioral sequences, you'll hit limits.

---

### 5. Klaviyo — Best for E-Commerce

Klaviyo is the dominant email platform for e-commerce, and for good reason. If you're running a Shopify, WooCommerce, or Magento store, Klaviyo is purpose-built for you in a way that Mailchimp (or any general-purpose email tool) isn't.

**What makes it exceptional:**
- Deep native integration with Shopify — product data, purchase history, browsing behavior
- Abandoned cart, browse abandonment, and post-purchase flows out of the box
- Revenue attribution shows exactly what each email campaign generated
- Predictive analytics: expected next purchase date, customer lifetime value, churn risk
- SMS and email in one platform with coordinated automation
- Pre-built flow templates for the most common e-commerce sequences

**Pricing:**
- Free: 250 contacts, 500 emails/month
- Email: $45/month for 1,000 contacts
- Email + SMS: $60/month for 1,000 contacts

**Best for:** E-commerce businesses on Shopify or WooCommerce. If you're selling physical products, Klaviyo is the industry standard.

**Watch out:** Klaviyo is expensive relative to general-purpose email tools. If you're not doing e-commerce, the revenue attribution and purchase-behavior features that justify the cost don't apply.

## The Verdict

The right Mailchimp alternative depends on who you are:

- **Creators, bloggers, coaches, solopreneurs** → **ConvertKit/Kit**. The subscriber model, creator tools, and clean automation are built for you specifically.
- **Businesses that need serious automation** → **ActiveCampaign**. The most powerful automation builder in this category. Requires investment to set up, pays off at scale.
- **Budget is the primary constraint** → **Brevo**. Free up to 300 emails/day, contact-count pricing is gone.
- **Mailchimp replacement with minimal friction** → **MailerLite**. Cheapest comparable feature set, easiest migration.
- **Shopify or WooCommerce store** → **Klaviyo**. Non-negotiable choice for e-commerce.

One thing to note: all five tools offer free migration assistance and data import. The technical switch is simpler than it sounds.
