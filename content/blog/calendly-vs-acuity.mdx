Calendly and Acuity are both excellent scheduling tools, but they're built for different types of businesses. If you pick the wrong one, you'll either be paying for features you don't need (Acuity for a simple consultant) or running into walls constantly (Calendly for a service business with packages and payments).

This comparison is direct: we'll tell you exactly who wins in each scenario.

---

## The Core Difference

**Calendly** is a meeting scheduler. It's designed for the use case of: "I need people to book time on my calendar." Sales calls, consultations, demos, interviews, coaching sessions. The workflow is book → remind → meet.

**Acuity** is an appointment booking system. It's designed for the use case of: "I need clients to book and pay for my services." Therapy sessions, photography shoots, fitness training, hair appointments. The workflow is book → pay → intake form → remind → appointment → follow-up.

That distinction matters enormously. If your product *is* the appointment, use Acuity. If the appointment is a step *toward* something else, use Calendly.

---

## Pricing Comparison

**Calendly:**
- Free: 1 event type, unlimited bookings
- Standard: $10/user/month (unlimited event types, workflows, payments)
- Teams: $16/user/month (routing, round-robin, team pages)
- Enterprise: Custom

**Acuity Scheduling:**
- No free plan
- Emerging: $20/month (1 user)
- Growing: $34/month (up to 6 users)
- Powerhouse: $61/month (up to 36 users)

**Winner on price: Calendly.** At $10/user/month vs. $20/month minimum for Acuity, Calendly is cheaper for small teams. For a solo user who just needs meetings, Calendly's free plan may be enough.

---

## Ease of Setup

**Calendly** can be set up in 20 minutes. Connect your Google or Outlook calendar, set your availability, create an event type, and share the link. That's it.

**Acuity** takes longer — typically 1–2 hours for a proper setup. You need to configure appointment types, set up payment processing, build intake forms, define your cancellation policy, and test the entire booking flow. More power means more configuration.

**Winner: Calendly.** Significantly faster to launch. Acuity rewards the setup time with more power, but if you want something live today, Calendly wins.

---

## Booking Experience for Clients

Both tools create a clean, mobile-friendly booking page. The visual experience is comparable.

**Calendly's client experience:**
- Client picks a meeting type, selects a time, confirms with name/email
- Optional: intake questions, redirect to a URL after booking
- Clean, fast, minimal

**Acuity's client experience:**
- Client picks a service type, selects a time
- Fills out intake form (as detailed as you need)
- Pays via Stripe, Square, or PayPal
- Receives confirmation with any attached documents
- Can reschedule/cancel within your rules

**Winner: Depends on use case.** For a sales call, Calendly's simplicity is an advantage. For a therapy intake or photography booking, Acuity's depth is an advantage.

---

## Payment Collection

**Calendly:** Accepts payments via Stripe and PayPal on paid plans. Works for simple session fees. No packages, no gift certificates, no deposit-only payment.

**Acuity:** Full payment system. Accepts Stripe, Square, and PayPal. Supports:
- Full payment at booking
- Deposits (client pays X% now, rest on arrival)
- Multi-session packages (buy 10 sessions, use over time)
- Gift certificates
- Subscriptions (recurring membership payments)
- Coupon codes

**Winner: Acuity.** It's not close. If you need anything more than "client pays the session fee at booking," Calendly doesn't have it.

---

## Intake Forms

**Calendly:** Basic. You can add questions to your booking form (short text, dropdown, checkbox). Fine for capturing a few details.

**Acuity:** Sophisticated. Custom form builder with multiple field types. You can set different intake forms for different appointment types, require certain fields, and collect detailed pre-appointment information. The intake form response is visible in the appointment record.

**Winner: Acuity.** If intake information matters to your work (therapists, fitness coaches, photographers, consultants who need pre-meeting context), Acuity is substantially better.

---

## Team Scheduling

**Calendly:**
- Round-robin: Distribute bookings evenly or by priority across team members
- Collective: Require multiple team members to be available for one meeting
- Team page: One link to show availability across your team
- Routing: Ask qualifying questions and route to the right person

**Acuity:**
- Multiple staff calendars under one account
- Each staff member has their own availability and services
- Front-desk style management of team appointments
- Client can choose their preferred staff member

**Winner: Depends.** Calendly is better for sales and meeting-style team scheduling (round-robin, routing). Acuity is better for service businesses with multiple practitioners where clients book specific people (e.g., "I want to book with Sarah, not Marcus").

---

## Integrations

**Calendly:** 100+ integrations. Google Meet, Zoom, Microsoft Teams (auto-creates meeting link). HubSpot, Salesforce, LinkedIn, Notion, Slack, Zapier, and more.

**Acuity:** Strong integrations but fewer. Zoom (auto-creates link), Google Analytics, Mailchimp, Zapier, QuickBooks, FreshBooks. Squarespace integration is seamless (they own Acuity).

**Winner: Calendly.** Particularly if you use a CRM — Calendly's HubSpot and Salesforce integrations are much deeper.

---

## No-Show Protection

**Calendly:** Automated email and SMS reminders. No credit card required feature is available but basic — no cancellation fees.

**Acuity:** Credit card on file (charge no-show fee), cancellation window enforcement (client can't cancel within X hours of appointment), charge for late cancellations. This is real protection, not just reminders.

**Winner: Acuity.** If no-shows cost you real money, Acuity's credit card on file and cancellation policies are meaningful.

---

## Who Should Use Calendly

You're the right candidate for Calendly if:

- **You schedule meetings, not appointments.** Sales calls, coaching sessions, interviews, demos.
- **Budget matters.** $10/month vs. $20/month is real, especially for teams.
- **You want simple.** Calendly takes 20 minutes to set up and just works.
- **You use HubSpot or Salesforce.** Calendly's CRM integrations are superior.
- **Your team needs round-robin or routing.** Calendly is purpose-built for this.
- **You're testing whether scheduling software helps.** The free plan is enough to start.

---

## Who Should Use Acuity

You're the right candidate for Acuity if:

- **Appointments are your product.** You're a coach, therapist, photographer, trainer, tutor, salon.
- **You need to collect payment at booking.** Especially packages, deposits, or gift certificates.
- **Intake forms matter.** You need client information before the appointment.
- **No-show protection is important.** Your time has direct dollar value — cancellations hurt.
- **You're on Squarespace.** Native integration is seamless.
- **You want clients to reschedule themselves.** Acuity's client self-service portal handles this cleanly.

---

## The Direct Answer

**Use Calendly if you work in meetings.** It's cheaper, faster to set up, and integrates better with most business tools.

**Use Acuity if you sell appointments.** It handles the complete service business workflow — booking, payment, intake, reminders, follow-up — better than anything else in this price range.

The mistake most people make is using Calendly for a service business because it's cheaper and simpler, then hitting walls when they can't collect deposits or sell packages. Spend the extra $10/month on Acuity if that's your situation. It'll save you frustration.
