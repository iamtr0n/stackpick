Scheduling software solves a specific, painful problem: the "what time works for you?" email chain that can take 4–6 exchanges before landing on a meeting. Good scheduling software eliminates that entirely.

But the market is crowded and the tools are very different. Calendly is great for simple booking links. Acuity is better for service businesses. HoneyBook bundles scheduling with contracts and payments. Square Appointments is built for in-person businesses.

Here's the breakdown.

---

## Quick Picks

- **Best overall:** Calendly (simple, powerful, works for most businesses)
- **Best for service businesses:** Acuity Scheduling
- **Best all-in-one for freelancers:** HoneyBook
- **Best for in-person/retail:** Square Appointments
- **Best for solopreneurs on a budget:** Book Like A Boss

## 1. Calendly — Best Overall

**Pricing:** Free (1 event type), Standard $10/user/month, Teams $16/user/month, Enterprise custom

Calendly is the category leader for good reason: it's fast to set up, integrates with everything, and the booking experience is clean for whoever's booking with you.

**What it does well:**
- Connect multiple calendars (Google, Outlook, iCloud) and block busy times automatically
- One-click scheduling links you paste into emails or add to your website
- Automated reminders and follow-ups reduce no-shows
- Round-robin scheduling for teams (distribute meetings evenly)
- Collective scheduling (require multiple team members to be available)
- Integrations with Zoom, HubSpot, Salesforce, Zapier, 100+ others

**Free plan:** Solid. One event type, unlimited bookings, Calendly branding. Enough to test whether scheduling software helps.

**Paid plans:** Standard ($10/user/month) unlocks unlimited event types, workflows (automated email/SMS reminders), payments via Stripe/PayPal, and branding removal. Teams adds routing, collective scheduling, and reporting.

**Honest limitation:** Calendly is primarily designed for meetings. It's not built for complex service bookings with packages, intake forms, contracts, or payments beyond simple session fees. For that, Acuity or HoneyBook is better.

**Best for:** Consultants, salespeople, coaches, agencies, and anyone who does a lot of calls and meetings.

## 2. Acuity Scheduling — Best for Service Businesses

**Pricing:** Emerging $20/month (1 user), Growing $34/month (6 users), Powerhouse $61/month (36 users)

Acuity (owned by Squarespace) is built for businesses that sell appointments as their product — coaches, therapists, photographers, hair salons, tutors, fitness trainers. The difference from Calendly: Acuity handles the whole client experience, not just the booking link.

**What Acuity does that Calendly doesn't:**
- **Client intake forms** — collect information before the appointment
- **Package management** — sell 5-session packages, gift certificates, subscriptions
- **Payments** — collect payment at booking via Stripe, Square, or PayPal
- **No-show protection** — require a card on file, charge for cancellations
- **Client self-service** — clients can reschedule or cancel themselves within your rules
- **Multiple appointment types** — group classes, recurring appointments, different durations

**Honest limitation:** Acuity is noticeably more complex to set up than Calendly. Expect 1–2 hours to configure everything correctly. Also, there's no free plan — $20/month minimum.

**Best for:** Service businesses where appointments are the product: coaches, therapists, photographers, salons, personal trainers, tutors.

## 3. HoneyBook — Best All-in-One for Freelancers

**Pricing:** Starter $19/month, Essentials $39/month, Premium $79/month (all plans unlimited projects)

HoneyBook goes beyond scheduling into full client management. It's built for freelancers and small service businesses that need to manage the complete client lifecycle: inquiry → booking → contract → invoice → payment → project delivery.

**Scheduling in HoneyBook:**
- Booking pages with embedded scheduling
- Intake forms attached to booking
- Automatic contract and invoice sending after booking
- Payment collection at booking

**What HoneyBook adds beyond scheduling:**
- Contracts and e-signatures (legally binding)
- Invoices and payment processing
- Project management and client portals
- Automation workflows (when client books → send contract → send invoice → etc.)

**Honest limitation:** HoneyBook is overkill if you just need scheduling. Use it when you want to replace 3–4 tools (scheduler + contracts + invoicing + project management) with one.

**Best for:** Freelancers and creative service businesses (photographers, designers, event planners, copywriters) who want to automate the entire client onboarding process.

## 4. Square Appointments — Best for In-Person Businesses

**Pricing:** Free for individuals, Plus $29/month (2–5 staff), Premium $69/month (6–10 staff)

Square Appointments is built for businesses where clients come to you: salons, barbershops, spas, tattoo studios, personal training studios, massage therapists.

**Key features:**
- Client-facing booking website (included)
- Point-of-sale integration (book and pay in one system)
- Staff scheduling and calendar management
- No-show protection with card-on-file
- Automated reminders via email and SMS
- Inventory management for product sales
- Square's payment processing (2.6% + 10¢ per tap/dip)

**Free plan is genuinely free** for individual practitioners. You just pay Square's payment processing fee. The free plan includes unlimited appointments, a booking site, and automated reminders.

**Honest limitation:** Square Appointments is deeply integrated with Square's payment ecosystem. If you process payments elsewhere, there's friction. Also not built for remote/virtual services — the whole system assumes in-person.

**Best for:** Brick-and-mortar service businesses, especially those already using Square for payments.

---

## 5. Book Like A Boss — Best Budget Option for Solopreneurs

**Pricing:** Lite $9/month, Basic $19/month, Unlimited $29/month

Book Like A Boss is the underdog on this list. It's less polished than Calendly and less powerful than Acuity, but it's affordable and covers the basics well.

**What it does:**
- Booking page with custom branding
- Multiple appointment types
- Payments via Stripe or PayPal
- Intake forms
- Automated emails and reminders
- Basic analytics

**Honest limitation:** The UI feels dated compared to Calendly. Integrations are limited (no native Zoom integration on the cheap plan, for example). Less reliable support than the larger players.

**Best for:** Solopreneurs on a tight budget who want more than Calendly's free tier without the price jump to Acuity.

## The Verdict

**Start with Calendly** if you primarily schedule meetings (calls, demos, consultations). The free plan is enough to start, and the paid plan at $10/month is reasonable. It's the most versatile tool on this list.

**Switch to Acuity** if you're a service business where clients book and pay for sessions, classes, or packages — and you need intake forms, cancellation policies, and package management.

**Use HoneyBook** if you want to replace your scheduler + contract tool + invoicing tool with one platform.

**Use Square Appointments** if you're a brick-and-mortar business that already uses Square or wants a free scheduling tool with built-in payments.

The best scheduling software is the one your clients actually book through. Set one up, share the link, and stop the email chains.
